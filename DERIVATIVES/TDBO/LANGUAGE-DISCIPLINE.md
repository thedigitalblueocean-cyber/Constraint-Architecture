# TDBO Language Discipline — Mandatory Enforcement

**Contributor:** The Digital Blue Ocean Ltd. (TDBO)  
**Date:** 25 March 2026  
**Source:** Watson boundary-alignment memo + 24 March 2026 hardening pass  

---

## Why This Exists

The 512/CVS primitives define **constraint behaviour and evidence mechanics** — not institutional compliance. Institutional interpretations remain external to the primitive and are determined by regulators, insurers, and auditors.

Using forbidden language in TDBO materials:
- Creates false claims of certification
- Conflates primitive definitions with regulatory outcomes
- Weakens legal defensibility under adversarial scrutiny
- Violates the upstream language standard established by Jon Watson

---

## Seven Forbidden Phrases

| Forbidden Phrase | Why Forbidden | Correct Replacement |
|-----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| "CVS certifies compliance" | CVS produces evidence, not certifications | "CVS produces independently verifiable cryptographic records of observation" |
| "512/CVS-compliant" (applied to TDBO systems) | Compliance is determined externally by regulators/auditors | "512/CVS-aligned derivative implementation" |
| "Validated by 512/CVS" | Validation is not a primitive function | "Constrained and evidenced by the 512/CVS stack" |
| "Institutionally compliant" (re: primitives) | Institutional interpretations remain external | "Enabling institutions to demonstrate constraint integrity to their own regulators" |
| "Requirement satisfied" (re: primitives) | Requirements are determined externally | "Structurally consistent with [specific requirement]" |
| "Only solution" | Not a canonical claim | "As of [date], TDBO's systematic analysis has identified no competing implementation combining all four layers" |
| "Six invariants" (without SOP labels) | Canonical labelling is mandatory | "SOP-1 through SOP-6" |

## Scope of Enforcement

Applies to **all TDBO materials**:
- Internal documents and SOPs
- External presentations and decks
- DIFC/DFSA regulatory submissions
- Insurer and bank proposals
- Client deliverables and white papers
- LinkedIn posts and public communications
- Source code headers and API documentation

---

## Permitted Language

✅ Primitives: describe only **constraint behaviour and evidence mechanics**  
✅ Compliance/validation: use only in relation to **specific deployments** and **external determinations** by regulators, insurers, or auditors  
✅ Competitive positioning: frame as scoped, dated, revisable finding — "As of [date], no competing implementation identified"

## Bilateral Enforcement — TDBO × STARGA

This language discipline applies equally to STARGA materials directed to TDBO clients, DIFC, DFSA, or other institutional parties.

Additional TDBO × STARGA specific rules:

| Forbidden | Correct Replacement |
|-----------|---------------------|
| "Three-layer architecture" | "Four-layer architecture (Layer 0 + Layers 1–3)" |
| "Nine invariants" (as canonical) | "SOP-1 through SOP-6 (Watson canonical); I-1–I-9 (STARGA subordinate enforcement layer)" |

STARGA's nine runtime enforcement invariants (I-1–I-9) are **derivative runtime enforcement mechanisms** implementing the Watson SOPs at machine level. They are not canonical 512/CVS invariants.
